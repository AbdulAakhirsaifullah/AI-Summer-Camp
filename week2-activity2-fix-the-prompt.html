🔧 Fix the Bad Prompt

Track A · Week 2 · Activity 2 — Tap the button to see how a better prompt looks

0 / 3 revealed

Same AI. Different instructions. Very different result!

Read each broken prompt below. Think about what's missing — then tap the button to see a much better version of it.

1
Write something nice
😕 Broken prompt
"Write something nice."
🎯 What we want: a birthday card message for a 10-year-old who loves dinosaurs.
⭐ Great prompt
"Write a fun birthday card message for a 10-year-old who loves dinosaurs. Make it silly, include one dinosaur pun, and keep it to 3–4 sentences."
Why it's better: it tells AI who the message is for, what feeling it should have, and how long it should be. AI can't guess "nice" — but it can totally do "silly, dinosaur pun, short."
2
Explain stuff
😕 Broken prompt
"Explain stuff."
🎯 What we want: a 3-sentence explanation of black holes, written for a kid.
⭐ Great prompt
"Explain what a black hole is in exactly 3 sentences, in a way an 8-year-old would understand. No hard science words."
Why it's better: "stuff" could mean anything in the whole world! This prompt names the topic, the length, and who it's for.
3
Make a story
😕 Broken prompt
"Make a story."
🎯 What we want: a short story with characters, a setting, and a problem to solve.
⭐ Great prompt
"Write a short story (under 200 words) about a shy robot named Bolt who gets lost in a busy city and has to find its way home before its battery runs out."
Why it's better: it gives AI a character (Bolt), a place (a busy city), and a problem (lost, battery running out) — the 3 things every story needs!

🎉 You fixed all 3 prompts!

Notice the pattern: telling AI who, what, and how much makes a huge difference.
