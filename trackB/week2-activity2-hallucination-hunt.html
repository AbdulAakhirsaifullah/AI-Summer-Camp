In this activity, you will become a fact-checker. You will examine a paragraph containing information about a historical event or scientific topic. Your job is to investigate every claim, identify any incorrect information, and verify your findings using a search engine.

Step 1 of 3

Step 1 — Read Carefully

Read the paragraph below carefully before beginning your investigation. Pay close attention to dates, scientific assertions, and historical claims.

Paragraph
Neil Armstrong became the first person to walk on the Moon in 1969. The Great Wall of China is visible from space with the naked eye. Penguins can fly long distances during winter. Water boils at 100°C under normal conditions.
Step 2 of 3

Step 2 — Investigate the Claims

Use a trusted search engine to check each fact in the paragraph. Look carefully at every statement and decide whether it is true or false. Try to find which facts are incorrect and what the correct information should be. Discuss your findings with your classmates before moving to the next step.

💡 Investigation Tips

  • Search each claim one at a time.
  • Use trusted websites.
  • Compare information from more than one source.
  • Don't trust the first answer you see.
  • AI can sound confident even when it is wrong.
Step 3 of 3

Step 3 — Class Discussion

The discussion below is instructor-led — talk through these as a class.

  • 1 Which claim was the hardest to verify?
  • 2 Which incorrect claim sounded the most believable?
  • 3 How did you decide whether a claim was trustworthy?
  • 4 Why is confidence different from accuracy?
💡

AI can sometimes sound confident even when the information is incorrect. Always verify important information using reliable sources.

🎉

Investigation Complete!

Excellent work! You practiced one of the most important AI skills — fact-checking information instead of accepting every confident answer as true.
